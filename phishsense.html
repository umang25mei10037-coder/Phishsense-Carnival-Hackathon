What Is Phishing?

A deceptive cyberattack where criminals disguise themselves as trustworthy entities to steal sensitive information.

🎣
🕵️

Identity Deception

Attackers impersonate banks, tech companies, and government agencies using near-perfect replicas of official communications.

⚡

Urgency Manipulation

Messages create artificial urgency — "Your account will be suspended in 24 hours" — to bypass rational thinking.

🔗

Malicious Links

Links lead to fake websites that harvest credentials, or trigger malware downloads upon clicking.

💰

Financial Exploitation

Stolen credentials are used for unauthorized transactions, identity theft, and account takeovers.

📋 Taxonomy

Types of Phishing

Attackers use many vectors. Know them all to stay protected across every channel.

📧

Email Phishing

Mass emails mimicking trusted organizations sent to thousands simultaneously, hoping a fraction clicks.

High Risk
🎯

Spear Phishing

Highly targeted attacks using personal information gathered from social media to appear legitimate and personal.

Critical Risk
🐋

Whaling

Executive-targeted attacks against CEOs, CFOs, and other C-suite leaders with high-value access.

Critical Risk
📱

Smishing

SMS-based phishing using fake text messages. "Your package is delayed — click here to confirm delivery."

Medium Risk
📞

Vishing

Voice phishing via phone calls. Attackers impersonate IRS agents, bank fraud departments, or tech support.

Medium Risk
🌐

Clone Phishing

Exact copies of legitimate emails with links replaced by malicious ones. Hard to detect without inspection.

High Risk
🔍

Search Engine Phishing

Fake websites ranked highly in search results. Users land on them thinking they're official sites.

Medium Risk
💬

Social Media Phishing

Fake profiles and messages on LinkedIn, Instagram, and Facebook used to extract personal information.

Moderate Risk

Report Cyber Phishing

Spotted a phishing attempt? Report it immediately to these official cybercrime authorities. Your report helps protect millions of others.

IND

National Cyber Crime Reporting Portal

Official Government of India portal for reporting cybercrime incidents including phishing, financial fraud, and social media crimes.

Visit Portal →
Helpline: 1930 (24x7)
Via online portal at cybercrime.gov.in
🔗 Report at cybercrime.gov.in
🇺🇸

FTC — Federal Trade Commission

Report phishing scams, spam, and fraudulent emails to the FTC to help them take action against cybercriminals.

reportphishing@apwg.org

UK's official cybercrime reporting center. Forward suspicious emails directly to their reporting service.
